Books and Spells

  • the Wise, Bingus. Gnomes, Gnomes, Gnomes: Utilitarian Applications for the Oft-Derided Garden Pest.
    • This novel is a primer on dealing with garden gnomes in fun and innovative ways. It has been heralded as one of the most forward-thinking treatises on the matter and, according to the world's leading gnome pundits (excluding myself, the most qualified of all, of course), has directly contributed to the Stinger's invention.
      Addendum: It is with great pleasure that I announce the third edition has been translated into Greek, Swiss, Arabic, Vietnamese, French, Portuguese, Spanish, and, for whatever reason, Latin.
  • the Wise, Bingus. Carnivals for Ants.
    • You don't need cauldrons, spells, and suspicious glances from voyeurs to practice your magic. Why not use what is already accessible? Ants are available everywhere; as such, they are the best things to practice on. This is the perfect entry into entomancy, insect enchantment, and team management (albeit with coersion).
  • the Wise, Bingus. You and Your Orb: A Beginner's Guide to Passable Clairvoyance.
    • Peering into the future is one of the most salient parts of magic. But just how is one to do so? Enter my novel. With beginner exercises and orb recommendations, you will soon join the ranks of acclaimed clairvoyants across history (though whether or not you'll be remembered is irrelevant and, sadly, quite unlikely). Start small with tomorrow's weather forecast. Move on to bigger things like employment opportunities. Lastly, entertain yourself with perfect bets on sports events. Dare to see which monarchs will live forever!
      • Note that it is highly discouraged and extremely dangerous (if only for how depressing it is) to prognosticate one's own future. I do not claim any irresponsibility for the inevitable fallout coinciding with your ungovernable curiosity.
  • Xylo, Marcus. Charms, Hexes, and Abject Buffoonery: The Life and Times of Bingus the Wise.
    • This book attempts to relay the storied and rich history of my life. It is... not terriffically well written, but at least the spirit is earnest.
